## Bio

Jack is a Data Scientist and Engineer with 10+ years of experience building data products people actually use: analytics platforms, ML systems, and production-grade data infrastructure. His work spans retail, e-commerce, and trend forecasting, where data is messy, questions are ambiguous, and everyone would like an answer *yesterday*. He is an optim-<u>*ist*</u> and optim-<u>*iser*</u>, applying both principles to everything from fitness and travel to [Navigating the AI-pocalypse](hugo-ref:blog/2026-03-01-a-survival-guide-to-redundancy.md).

[Redundancy in October 2025](hugo-ref:blog/2026-03-01-a-survival-guide-to-redundancy.md) was the catalyst to [Building this site](hugo-ref:blog/2026-03-01-a-survival-guide-to-redundancy.md): part notebook, part portfolio, and part evidence that side projects can come to fruition.

## How Jack works

I think in terms of systems, constraints, and trade-offs — shaped by a computer science and mathematics background, and by working in an industry where decisions are fast, high-stakes, and feedback loops are slow. I focus on building platforms that reduce cognitive load and create leverage — making the *right way* the easy way. Some rules to live by:
- Impact over elegance  
- Simplicity over complexity  
- Long-term maintainability over short-term optimisation  

##### Leadership style

Jack aligns most strongly **servant leadership**; not as a philosophy but a practical strategy to create a multiplier effect. He doesn't believe in choosing between technical and managerial tracks. Strong technical leadership requires both and so far he's spent his career staying hands-on while building and leading teams.

##### Traits of the best
The best engineers and data scientists I’ve had the pleasure of working with tend to:
- Run towards hard problems  
- Understand *why* before acting  
- Take ownership of fixing problems  
- Leave things in a better state than they found them
- Communicate clearly and often
- Adapt to their audience  
- Invest in the people around them  

##### Strengths & weaknesses

**Cross-functional communication**
- Facilitating technical discussions across audience (infra, DS/DE, product, business)  
- Aligning teams toward shared outcomes  

**Platform mindset**
- Enablement over ownership  
- Reducing cognitive load  
- Creating scalable systems  

**Pragmatism**
- Focus on impact  
- Use Occam’s razor  
- Balance cost, speed, and trust  
- Prioritise maintainability  

##### Weaknesses

**A healthy Sceptic**
- Challenge assumptions and explore edge cases  
- Can be perceived as resistance  

*Mitigation:*  
Make intent explicit (“pressure-testing, not blocking”), anchor discussions in outcomes and trade-offs  

**More of a listener**
- Prefer forming a considered opinion before speaking  
- Can appear disengaged  

*Mitigation:*  
Share thinking earlier and iterate in the open
