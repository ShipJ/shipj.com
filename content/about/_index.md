## Bio

I’m a Data Scientist and Engineer with over a decade of experience building data products that actually get used — predictive models, analytics platforms, and production-grade data systems, primarily in retail and e-commerce.

In early 2025, I was made redundant. Instead of waiting for the next role, I decided to build the consulting practice I’d been planning for years. This site is part of that process — a place to document ideas, share what I’ve learned, and show how I approach real-world data problems.

---

## Professional Background

I spent 8 years at WGSN, the world’s leading trend authority, where I most recently led Data Science and Engineering. My role was to help evolve a traditional publishing business into a data-driven product company — building the systems, teams, and culture required to make that shift real.

Over that time, I worked across a wide range of problems:
- Computer vision  
- Search  
- Fraud detection  
- Churn and lifetime value modelling  
- Time-series forecasting  
- Large-scale data migrations  

The consistent theme was turning messy, ambiguous data into reliable, production-ready systems.

My career started in business intelligence (Tableau) and evolved into full end-to-end ownership:

```sql

ingestion → transformation (dbt) → modelling → production

```

I built and launched customer-facing data products (including *Trendcurve*), and increasingly focused on designing **data platforms** — systems that enable teams to make decisions, measure outcomes, and continuously improve.

---

## How I Work

I think in terms of systems, constraints, and trade-offs — shaped by a computer science and mathematics background, and by working in an industry where decisions are fast, high-stakes, and feedback loops are slow.

My approach is pragmatic:
- Impact over elegance  
- Simplicity over complexity  
- Long-term maintainability over short-term optimisation  

I focus on building platforms that reduce cognitive load and create leverage — making the *right way* the easy way.

---

## Leadership

I align with **servant leadership**, not as a philosophy but as a practical strategy to create a multiplier effect.

I don’t believe in choosing between technical and managerial tracks. Strong technical leadership requires both. I’ve spent my career staying hands-on while building and leading teams.

## What I Value in Engineering

The best engineers and data scientists I’ve worked with tend to:

- Run towards hard, ambiguous problems  
- Communicate clearly and often, adapting to their audience  
- Be deeply curious and seek to understand *why* before acting  
- Take ownership and fix problems properly  
- Invest in growing the people around them

## Weaknesses

**Sceptical by nature**
- Challenge assumptions and explore edge cases  
- Can be perceived as resistance  

*Mitigation:*  
Make intent explicit (“pressure-testing, not blocking”), anchor discussions in outcomes and trade-offs  

**More of a listener**
- Prefer forming a considered opinion before speaking  
- Can appear disengaged  

*Mitigation:*  
Share thinking earlier and iterate in the open
